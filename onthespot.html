Overview

  • What is on the Spot? The dog training app’s native design features accurate information from knowledgeable trainers and animal experts for dog enthusiasts who want to learn about and train their dog.
  • Role UX/UI Designer
  • Scope User Research, UX Design, Visual Design, Prototyping, Usability Testing
  • Tools Figma, UsabilityHub, Lookback, Adobe Creative Suites (InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop)
  • Timeline 24 weeks, April - Sept 2023

My Iterative Process

Phase 1 - Understand

The Why

As a dog owner myself, with often little time to dedicate to in-person training I wondered how many other people struggled with the same problem.
  • Over 23 million Americans — nearly 1 in 5 — adopted a pet during the pandemic.*
  • Top reasons why people don’t train with a professional: time, budget, and location.

    • *according to ASPCA

    Phase 2 - Research

    Research Goals

  • To better understand user feelings and behaviors around their dog.
  • To better understand user thoughts and attitudes towards dog training.
  • To understand current methods dog owners use to train their dog and why.
  • Documenting user pain points with training their dog.

    • User Survey

    I started the process with a user survey, which allowed me to gather initial and honest insights from 34 potential users. This large group feedback was extremely helpful in determining whether I was on the right track in my efforts to understand user objectives and pain points.

    User Interviews

    After the survey, I conducted 6 moderated user interviews with a range of dog training experience, from novices to active trainers, some with one dog and others with multiple. These interviews provided valuable insights into each user’s perspective, enhancing my understanding.

    *interview script, interview, and summary

    Affinity Mapping

    After completing surveys and interviews, I consolidated all the data into an affinity map to organize the information and identify patterns.

    Key Insights

    1. Users see their dog as family and would be willing to spend time and money if it benefited their dog (61% users spend over half the day with their dog).
      • Validated the need for the app
    2. Users want the same benefits of an in-person trainer (only 3% of users had time for an in-person session).
      • Ability to communicate through video call with their trainer
    3. The app needs to have more appeal than free videos online (90% have tried).
      • Provide assistance on videos (step-by-step, tips, difficulty levels)
    4. Users get frustrated which prevents them from continuing to train (90% have attempted and failed due to motivation).
      • App needs to be simple and intuitive

    User Personas

    Once I had collected insights from user research, I proceeded to develop personas to continue to understand and empathize with our users.

    I created three distinct personas, each with unique desires, requirements, and situations. However for the purpose of this case study, I will primarily be focused on Louise.

    Louise

    • She/Her
    • 21-29
    • Single
    • Austin, TX

    Wants and Needs

    • Wants to feel confident taking her dog in public
    • Wants to be able to take her dog outside without having a negative interaction with other dogs
    • Wants to train on her schedule

    Frustrations

    • Finds it difficult to train alone
    • Has attempted training and feels discouraged about it

    Motivations

    • Give herself and her dog a happier life

    Journey Mapping

    The next step involved crafting a journey map that illustrates Louise’s experience as she navigates the process of achieving her goal which is to connect with a trainer.

    Phase 3 - Ideate

    Sitemap

    Following the creation of user flows, the next step was thinking about the entire app structure. I conducted an open card sort with 13 users to gather insight on how they envisioned the apps layout.

    Card Sorting

    We conducted an open cart sorting study using OptimalSort. The purpose of this study was to provide our team with insights into how users categorize items to help us organize the architecture and content of our app and its features.

    Challenge

    We had a low number of matches among users and some cards being added to an “uncategorized” pile. This indicated confusion in the content and labeling.

    Updated Sitemap

    Iterations of the sitemap were created based on user feedback from card sorting, and we also made changes as we developed the product and ran into functionality problems.
    • Reduced the number of tabs from 5 to 4 with the elimination of the homepage.
      • Users found the homepage unnecessary and similar to videos tab
    • Simplifying the content and labeling with more direct and simple language.
      • (e.i. Find a Trainer to Trainers and Messages to Inbox)
    • User assessment is part of the onboarding process but can be accessed through profile.
